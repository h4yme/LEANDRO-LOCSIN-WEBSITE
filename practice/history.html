HISTORY OF THE SCHOOL

WHO IS LEANDRO V. LOCSIN?

A home of productive citizens, Leandro Locsin Integrated School is named after a National Artist, a Filipino architect, and an interior designer, Mr. Leandro Valencia Locsin. Giving a view from whom the school is named after, Mr. Locsin is distinguished for his use of concrete, floating volume and simplistic design in his various projects. He was proclaimed a National Artist of the Philippines for Architecture in the year 1990. With the spirit of love and care to our dear learners, the school’s humble beginning had become an agent of nurturing young minds towards reaching the supreme goal of our cherished administrative officers and educators which is life transformations to our precious Locsinians. Indeed, LVLSHS symbolizes a domain of learning and a home for everybody.

His works are known not just here in the Philippines, but even in the international market.

THE HUMBLE BEGINNINGS

2016

Looking back, in 2016, the journey of Leandro Locsin Integrated School started. It was when Dr. Grace A. Tariman, the principal of Maligaya High School and the late Schools Division Superintendent, Helen Go, envisioned a senior high school which catered the incoming Grade 11 and Grade 12 students. Primarily, Dr. Carolyn C. Simon served as the Assistant to the Principal on the first year of LLIS’s endeavor.

Mrs. Arlene G. Sandoval, English Department Head in MHS also made a difference in contribution to the existence of Leandro Locsin Integrated School.

The first batch of SHS students were homed within Maligaya Elementary School, situated at Barangay Pasong Putik, Maligaya Subdivision, Novaliches, Quezon City. The school started with 23 number of ICT-Animation learners under the supervisory of Dr. Simon.
